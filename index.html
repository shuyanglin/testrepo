Cooking is therapeutic.

Cooking is therapeutic. It’s the quality time I get to spend with myself after all day mingling with colleagues and friends. It’s the loving time I get to focus on making a delicious meal for my partner. Although not the best chef in the world, all I can do is home cooking – but it is absolutely delicious! I make really good ginseng chicken soup. It’s super healthy and really tasty.

"What is the most efficient way to have more control in cooking?"

I know there’s always room for improvement and it’s not that hard if I put in time and effort into it. I would be making really clear and golden chicken soup while keeping the flavor rich. However, going to cooking classes seems to be too far from what I need, and browsing through those online materials is also time consuming. Forcing myself to take either of those approaches would just find myself end up collecting ways I have tried but given up. What is the most efficient way to have more control in cooking?

simply comparing with yourself

My team has been working on crafting the best environment for people to have more control in cooking and therefore be natural and creative in their kitchen. After lots of interviews, ideations, prototypes, testings, and dozens of iterations, we found out the best way of gaining a skill is not about finding the best teachers, buying the best materials, but simply comparing with yourself.

“Start, stop, continue retrospective”

Learning is all about doing it, reflecting and repeating. Cooking is the same! It’s about cooking, having a think, and making it again next time. It is really important to retrospect and take the benefit of hindsighting. You will find out what you want to add to, remove from, or keep in your own cooking – just like the “Start, stop, continue retrospective”. If you find yourself stuck at a certain point, just do something new. Don’t start over again. Just make a small change and see how it goes. You will find the answers very soon.

Values

  • Auto Record

Moment records every moment of your cooking for you. It recognizes when you start cooking, records automatically and archives all your cooking for later review.

  • Instant Playback

Moment texts you the playback every time you finish cooking. The best timing for retrospective is right after cooking.

  • Nimble Retrospect

Moment helps you to retrospect and hindsight efficiently. It gives you a 10x playback which makes it the most nimble way to debrief what makes the delicious dish in front of you.

  • Efficient Preparation

Moment provides you just enough information to get ready quickly before cooking. Play back the dish you want to cook again, that playback will set you ready straight away.
